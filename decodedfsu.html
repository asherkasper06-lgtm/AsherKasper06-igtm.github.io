DecodedFSU — Overview

Student media-literacy concept | Pattern recognition for propaganda and manipulative framing.

← Back to Portfolio Goals Audience Methods Examples Outcomes

DecodedFSU is a student-run awareness concept that teaches fast, practical ways to spot manipulation in social feeds. Instead of debating politics, it focuses on how messages work: visual tactics, euphemisms, symbol swaps, and emotional framing. The hypothesis: when students learn the pattern, the propaganda loses power.

Goals

  • Give students a tiny, memorable set of “decoder tools.”
  • Reduce impulsive reposting of unverified claims.
  • Model neutral, method-first analysis that applies across issues.

Audience

FSU undergraduates who use Instagram/TikTok as primary news spaces and want to avoid being manipulated online.

Methods

  • Post format: high-contrast, minimal slides engineered for 5–10 second reads.
  • Pattern teaching: call out tactics by name (e.g., loaded labels, cherry-picked visuals, false equivalence).
  • Source hygiene: every example references open-source posts already circulating in student feeds.
  • Value frame: empowerment over moralizing; “decode before you repost.”

Example Patterns (illustrative)

  • Symbol swap: replaces a stigmatized emblem with a normalized stand-in to launder meaning.
  • Euphemism chain: softens violent or exclusionary ideas with neutral/positive wording.
  • Emotional framing: zooms on imagery that primes outrage or pity to bypass scrutiny.
  • False balance: presents asymmetrical claims as equal to suggest controversy where there is none.

Anticipated Outcomes

  • Higher recognition of manipulative patterns in campus social feeds.
  • More cautious sharing behavior; increased demand for citations and context.
  • Reusable slides that faculty/organizations can reference for media-literacy sessions.
